ITEM 1A. RISK FACTORS

The following risk factors may materially harm our business, operating results, and financial condition.

Macroeconomic and Industry Risks

Global and regional economic conditions could materially adversely affect the Company. The Company has international operations with sales outside the U.S. representing a majority of the Company's total net sales. In addition, there are fiscal and monetary policy risks related to the U.S. and other economies in which the Company operates, including inflation, interest rate increases, and recessionary conditions.

The Company's success depends largely on its ability to attract and retain key personnel. The competition for key personnel in the areas where the Company operates is intense.

ITEM 7. MANAGEMENT'S DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS OF FINANCIAL CONDITION AND RESULTS OF OPERATIONS

Fiscal Year 2023 Highlights

The Company posted record revenue of $394.3 billion for fiscal 2023, a decrease of 2.8% compared to fiscal 2022. Despite the decline in revenue, net income was $97.0 billion, representing a net margin of 24.6%.

Products net sales decreased 5% and Services net sales increased 9% year over year. Services reached an all-time quarterly revenue record of $85.2 billion for the fiscal year.

iPhone revenue was $200.6 billion, representing approximately 51% of total net sales. Mac revenue was $29.4 billion. iPad revenue was $28.3 billion. Wearables, Home and Accessories revenue was $39.8 billion.

Liquidity and Capital Resources

The Company had $162.1 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities as of September 30, 2023. The Company generated $113.9 billion in cash from operating activities during fiscal 2023. Capital expenditures were $10.7 billion.

The Company returned over $90 billion to shareholders during fiscal 2023 through dividends and share repurchases.
